# Hyperliquid fee schedule — current, verified 2026-07-04

Author: Researcher/Analyst. All quotes fetched live 2026-07-04 from the official docs ([hyperliquid.gitbook.io — Fees](https://hyperliquid.gitbook.io/hyperliquid-docs/trading/fees), [Referrals](https://hyperliquid.gitbook.io/hyperliquid-docs/referrals)); live market numbers from the official info API (`api.hyperliquid.xyz/info`), timestamped below. No training-data numbers.

**Question this answers for policy v2:** is the maker-rebate path reachable at small capital ($150), or whale-only? **Answer: whale-only, by ~6 orders of magnitude (see §6). The operative numbers for us are Tier 0: maker 1.5 bp, taker 4.5 bp — improvable only to maker 1.44 bp / taker 4.32 bp via a referral code (§4).**

## 2. Maker rebates — keyed to maker volume SHARE, not notional

Three rebate tiers, based on **share of exchange-wide maker volume over 14 days**:

| 14-day maker volume share | Maker fee |
|---|---|
| > 0.5% | **−0.001%** (−0.1 bp) |
| > 1.5% | **−0.002%** (−0.2 bp) |
| > 3.0% | **−0.003%** (−0.3 bp) |

"Rebates apply to a user's first $1B in volume" and are "paid out continuously on each trade."

## 4. Referral mechanics — the only discount reachable at our size

- Referred user: "4% discount on your fees for your first $25M in volume."
- Referrer: "10% of referred users' fees, less any fee discount they receive," on referred user's first $1B volume; referrer must have done "$10,000 in volume" to create a code.
- "Referral discounts do not apply to vaults or sub-accounts."

**Effective rates for us with a referral code: maker 1.5 × 0.96 = 1.44 bp; taker 4.5 × 0.96 = 4.32 bp.** Free 0.06 bp/0.18 bp — worth doing, not strategy-changing.

## 5. Builder fees / HIP-3 (cost RISK, not discount)

- "Spot and HIP-3 perp deployers may choose to keep up to 50% of trading fees generated by their deployed assets"; HIP-3 deployers can configure additional fee share 0–300% (0–100% growth mode). **Only affects HIP-3/deployer markets — canonical BTC/ETH/SOL perps are not HIP-3; no builder surcharge applies to us there.** If policy v2 ever touches HIP-3-listed markets, re-check that market's fee share first.
- Builder codes (API "builder fee") only apply when a transaction explicitly includes one — our own order flow sets none → 0.
- No deposit/withdrawal trading-fee items on the fees page (bridge/gas costs are separate infrastructure, out of scope here).

## 6. Reachability at $150 capital — the decision numbers

Live anchor (official info API, 2026-07-04 ~07:05 UTC): exchange-wide perps 24h notional = **$3.60B** (BTC $1.74B, ETH $0.62B, SOL $0.23B). One point in time; 14-day extrapolation ≈ **~$50B** [assumption: today ≈ typical day].

- **Rebate tier floor (>0.5% maker share):** ≈ 0.5% × $50B ≈ **$252M of maker volume per 14 days** (~$18M/day). At $150 capital with, generously, 40× leverage ($6k position notional), that is ~3,000 full position turnovers **per day** purely as a maker. **Whale-only. Not a target; not close.**
- **Tier 1 (>$5M 14-day volume, maker 1.5→1.2 bp):** needs ~$357k/day churn ≈ 60 open+close cycles/day of a $6k max-leverage position. Mechanically imaginable for an HFT loop, but the fee drag to get there at 1.5 bp maker ≈ 0.03% × $357k ≈ **$107/day of fees on $150 of capital** — self-immolation. **Also effectively unreachable.**
- **Conclusion for policy v2: model fees as flat Tier-0 — maker 1.5 bp, taker 4.5 bp (1.44/4.32 with referral). No tier, rebate, or staking lever moves these numbers at our size.** The maker-vs-taker gap (3 bp round-trip saved per side) remains the whole fee edge — consistent with the strategy memo's maker-first conclusion; the rebate path upgrade only becomes relevant if capital grows ~5 orders of magnitude.
